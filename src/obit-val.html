Valerie H. Adkins

Valerie H. Adkins, 50, of 19226 Mulligan Lane, Meadville, died Tuesday, May 26, 1998, at Meadville Medical Center.

She was born in Bronx, N.Y., July 25, 1947. She was adopted by Loren and Maude Adkins.

She graduated from Conneaut Valley High School.

She worked as a graphic artist at The Meadville Tribune until she became ill and had since worked as a writer, graphic designer and provider of computer technical support.

She published a book on graphic design, a number of articles and short stories. She wrote a nature column for a Maine newspaper and had been writing a genealogy column called Roots of the Matter for The Tribune.

She was a member of Unitarian Universalist Church. She also was a former organist, choir director and soloist in churches in Maine and New York City.

Survivors include a longtime companion, Mary-Lib Whitney of Meadville; a daughter, Alice Thomas of Saegertown; two sons, James Muma of Saegertown and Jack Muma of Georgia; 10 grandchildren; a sister, Charlotte Hanraham of New York; a brother, Kevin Hanraham of New York; several nieces and nephews. She was preceded in death by her parents.

There will be no calling hours.

A memorial service will be June 8 at 7 p.m. at Unitarian Universalist Church, 346 Chestnut St., Meadville, with Josh Searle-White of the church officiating.

Burial will be private.

Arrangements are under the direction of White-Cool Funeral Home, 1405 Main St., Conneautville.

The family suggests memorials be made to Cancer Services of Crawford County, PO Box 1272, Meadville, Pa. 16335; or Unitarian Universalist Church Organ Fund, 346 Chestnut St., Meadville.
