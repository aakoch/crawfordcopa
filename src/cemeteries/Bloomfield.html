Name:  Bisbee Cemetery
From Eugene F. Throop’s index:  Located near the junction of Twp.Rts. 812 and 905, at the south-east corner of a grove of pines.  The area is now a state game lands, and the tombstone were removed to Shreve Ridge Cemetery.  The foundations of the house and barn are still visible, and myrtle marks the spot where the cemetery was located.

Name:  Bloomfield Family Cemetery
See Find A Grave (called “Bloomfield Burying Ground”)
From Eugene F. Throop’s index:  Located at the junction of Leg.Rt. 20084 and Twp.Rt. 826 (Cemetery Road), one-half mile west of S.R. 8 at Page’s Corners.  The cemetery is kept up pretty well, thanks to the Boy Scouts.  Some of the earliest pioneers are buried here.  An index was published in an Erie, Pa. newspaper genealogical column in the 1920s.  Toward the back of the cemetery there is a row of five identical small stones, two of these blank and the other three reading Price, Marsh, and Bryan.  Forty-three burials for the period 1814 – 1951.  Indexed 27 Oct. 1970:

Name:  Lincoln Cemetery (1)
From Eugene F. Throop’s index:  Located off Leg.Rt. 20083 (Shreve Ridge Road), about one mile north of Lincolnville, and 150 yards north-west of the first road to the left.  It is by a very large dead elm just north of gully, 100 yard off the road.  There is a large patch of myrtle, with the tree within the patch; there are indications that there may have been more than one burial.  Indexed 30 Nov. 1970:
Hamilton, John, d. 2 Feb. 1868 aged 70y 10m 29d; f.s. J.H.; epitaph

Name:  Lincoln Cemetery (2)
From Eugene F. Throop’s index:  Located in Lincolnville, near the junction of Leg.Rt. 20084 and Twp.Rt.[?] 190, beside a tool shed, on the Seth Lincoln homestead still farmed by Lincoln descendants.  To find the cemetery, inquire at the farmhouse on the corner.  Indexed 29 Oct. 1970:

(dau.) Mariann A., dau. of Seth C. & Lucinda, d. 17 July 1843 aged 11y 6m

Name:  Loomis Cemetery
See Find A Grave
From Eugene F. Throop’s index:  Modern cemetery, with few burials before 1920; 275 marked burials ranging from 1840 to 1982.  Indexed Oct. 1971, with additions from Meadville Tribune for 1971 through 1982.

Name:  (John) Mayer Farm Cemetery
From Eugene F. Throop’s index:  Located on Twp.Rt. 857 at the peak of a hill overlooking Chapinville.  Only myrtle remains; the tombstones may have been moved to Chapinville Cemetery or Loomis Cemetery or perhaps destroyed.  According to the Veterans Affairs Office, the ashes of Isaac Bechtold, a W.W.I veteran, were scattered here; he was born 5 Aug. 1889, and died 11 May 1950.


Name:  Merchant Farm Cemetery
From Eugene F. Throop’s index:  Located along S.R. 77, on top of a bank almost across from its intersection with Twp.Rt. 834.  There is a Revolutionary War veteran marker, but no tombstone.
Fuller, Ephraim

Name:  Mount Pleasant Cemetery
See Find A Grave
From Eugene F. Throop’s index:  Located at the junction of Leg.Rts. 20126 and 20093, two miles east of Tillotson Corners (along S.R. 8).  It is along the road and quite visible.  Many tombstones have been added in recent years giving only names and no dates; two such stones are illegible, and one said only “Iva.”  Indexed 8 Dec. 1970.

Name:  Potter–Blakeslee Cemetery
From Eugene F. Throop’s index:  Located about one-half mile south-west of Mount Pleasant Cemetery, and 200 yards south of Leg.Rt. 20093.  It is surrounded on three sides by fields, and visible from the road except during the summer.  It is enclosed within an iron fence and partially abandoned.  THe Blakeslee family here is related to the Blakeslees in Mount Pleasant Cemetery.  Indexed 1970 and 26 Sept. 1971:

Name:  Riceville Cemetery
See Find A Grave
From Eugene F. Throop’s index:  Located at Riceville just off the east side of S.R. 77.  This cemetery received good care and tombstones from several small family cemeteries have been moved here.  846 marked burials, beginning with Thomas Mitchell in 1837.  Indexed in Oct. 1975, with additional data from the Meadville Tribune of 1972 through Jan. 1983.

Name:  Shreve Cemetery
From Eugene F. Throop’s index:  Located about 200 yards behind a house and barn located at the junction of Twp.Rt. 831 and S.R. 8, beneath a pine tree in a cow pasture.  It can be found by following a farmer’s lane behind the barn.  It is in poor condition.  See Luther P. Allen, Genealogy and History of the Shreve Family (1901).
