     Crawford County was formed 12 March 1800 and in the 1800 federal census, completed a year later, 2,361 inhabitants were counted in 487 households.  Those census returns are transcribed below from the microfilmed copies.1

     The census provides the name of only the head of household.   Free white persons within each household were enumerated by sex and age, as follows: males under age ten, ten to fifteen, sixteen to twenty-five, twenty-six to forty-four, and forty-five or more years of age; females under age ten, ten to fifteen, sixteen to twenty-five, twenty-six to forty-four, and forty-five or over.   Two additional columns show the number of other free persons (excluding untaxed Indians), and slaves.
